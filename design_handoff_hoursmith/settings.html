Section 06 — Settings

One endless page doing five jobs at once.

You're right that Settings is the messiest surface. It isn't the fields — those are thoughtful. It's the architecture: a hero, a Setup Wizard, and a Diagnostics panel (which largely repeat each other) all stack on top of a six-fieldset mega-form with editors nested three levels deep. There's no navigation, status lives in four different places, and a returning user who just wants to flip the theme scrolls past the entire first-run wizard to get there. Below: why it reads as a mess, then a redesign that keeps 100% of the fields and behaviour — verified in a ledger at the bottom.

Mess 01

The Wizard and Diagnostics say the same thing twice

Both panels render status badges, a "run checks" button, Dashboard/Reports readiness, the Jira access-path guidance, and "jump to section" links — before you reach a single field. Two large cards, one message.

Mess 02

No navigation on a very tall page

Six fieldsets stack vertically with no rail, no anchors you can see, no sense of "where am I / what's left." The only way through is the scrollbar.

Mess 03

Status is scattered across four places

Per-section Test buttons, per-service badges, the wizard's step states, and a form-status bar all report readiness independently. No single source of truth.

Mess 04

"Services" is a fieldset hiding a whole app

One optional section contains GitLab, RescueTime, three calendar-feed types, a calendar-mappings editor, and a team-absence-assignment editor — each with its own composer. It needs room to breathe, not a single accordion-less block.

Mess 05

First-run setup and everyday maintenance are the same view

The wizard is invaluable on day one and pure noise on day 400. A returning admin who wants to add one teammate or toggle CSV columns has to wade through "Connect to Jira → run a check → verify and save" every single time. Setup and settings are two different jobs wearing one page.
