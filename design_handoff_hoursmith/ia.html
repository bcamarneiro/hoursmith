Section 02 — Structure · Information Architecture

The app has three names for two screens.

You felt an incongruence between Dashboard, Reports, my timesheet and my team's timesheet — and you're right. It isn't a vibe; it's visible in the router. The vocabulary drifted across three iterations and never got reconciled. Here's exactly what's tangled, why, and the clean structure that resolves it.

2.1

Straight from App.tsx and Navigation.tsx. The nav advertises three places — but four different names all resolve to a single screen, and that screen's component is named after a fifth.

2.3

Why this is the right cut

The mistake

Organised by subject

The app named places after whose hours you're looking at — my timesheet, my team's timesheet, a team page. But "me vs team" is one toggle's worth of difference, not two destinations. So the same features got built twice and the names multiplied.

my timesheetvsteam timesheet
The fix

Organise by intent

People arrive in one of two moods: "close my own week" or "review and export." That's the real fork. Make it the navigation. Then whose hours and which period are just filters on the review surface.

close · dovsreview · analyse
2.4

Every name, reconciled

A one-line ruling for each term in play today — so there's no ambiguity when this hits the codebase.

TodayBecomesVerdictRationale
Dashboard My Week Rename "Dashboard" is a generic catch-all. "My Week" names the scope (mine) and the job (this week) at a glance, and reads clearly against Reports.
Reports (route /reports) Reports Keep The right word for the review-and-export surface. It just needs to absorb the other four names instead of hiding behind them.
TimesheetPage (component) ReportsPage Rename Component name should match what it renders. The "timesheet" mental model is what started the drift — retire it from the code.
/team · /timesheet → /reports Merge Keep as silent redirects so old links survive, but drop them from the vocabulary. They're aliases, not places.
TeamPage.tsx — Delete An orphan stub that only redirects. Dead weight that keeps "Team" alive as a phantom destination.
"My team's timesheet" Reports · People: Team Merge Not a screen — a filter state. Becomes the default People filter when you lead a team.
"My timesheet" Reports · People: Me Merge Same surface, People filter set to yourself. Solo users land here by default and never see an empty "Team".
Weekly / Monthly toggle Reports · Period Keep Stays as the Period filter — but only lives in Reports now, so "weekly" no longer means two different things in two places.
2.5

The test: "where do I go?"

A clean IA answers this instantly for every user. After the change, it does.

"I forgot to log Thursday and it's Friday afternoon."
→ go to
My Week

Your current week, your gaps, fill and close. The solo freelancer lives here and may never need anything else.

"It's the 1st — I need the team's month as a CSV for the client."
→ go to
Reports

Set Period = Month, People = Team, export. Trends, presets and the consistency check all live in this one surface.

This also fixes the solo-vs-agency tension: a solo freelancer sees My Week + a Reports view quietly defaulted to "Me," with no confusing empty "Team." An agency lead gets the same two doors, with Reports defaulting to the whole team. One structure, both audiences.
