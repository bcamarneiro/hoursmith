Section 05 — Logo · Forge direction

Six ways to forge the mark.

All six live in the Forge system — iron & ember, Bricolage Grotesque, the craft the name promises. They range from literal (an anvil) to pure monogram (a struck "H"). Each is shown as a full lockup plus the contexts that decide a logo: scaled down to a favicon, reversed, and one-colour. None uses gradients or fine detail that would collapse at 16px.

Struck H

01

An ember "H" stamped into an iron tile, with a single strike-spark at the corner. The cleanest, most app-native mark — reads instantly as a tile and a monogram at once.

Billet H

02

Two iron posts joined by a glowing ember bar — raw stock being worked, hot, with sparks flying off the strike. The most name-true mark: it literally shows hours being forged, and it's an open monogram that needs no container.

Best for: distinctive & ownable
App Mono
Hoursmith

Anvil

03

The literal tool of the trade, drawn as a clean geometric silhouette with an ember spark landing on the horn. The most explicit nod to "smith" — warm and unmistakable, though less abstract than the monograms.

Best for: friendly & literal
App Light
Hoursmith

Maker's Stamp

04

An "H" punched into a hex seal — a smith's maker's mark, the stamp of provenance on finished work. The hexagon adds an industrial, bolt-head precision that suits the "numbers your client won't question" promise.

Best for: trust & provenance
App Light
Hoursmith

Strike Spark

05

No letterform — just the spark of hammer on hot metal, a four-point strike with a second smaller flash. The most energetic and abstract option; flexes well as a standalone icon and a "loading / working" motif in-app.

Best for: energetic & flexible
App Mono
Hoursmith

Wordmark + Spark

06

No icon at all — the wordmark carries it, with "smith" in ember and a single spark punctuating the end. The most grown-up, least gimmicky route; pairs with a derived "Hs" tile for the app icon and favicon.

Best for: minimal & mature
