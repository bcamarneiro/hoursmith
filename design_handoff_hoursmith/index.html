◆ The good

Real craft underneath

Two clear surfaces, a thoughtful token system, dark mode, PWA, an honest founder story. The information architecture is sound. This is not a weekend project.

◆ The problem

It looks auto-generated

Inter, indigo #4f46e5, slate grays and 135° gradients are the default palette of every AI-scaffolded SaaS. The brand is invisible.

◆ The opportunity

The name is the brand

"Hoursmith" already promises craft, forging, precision tooling. Nothing on screen earns that word. That gap is the entire brief.

Section 01 — Diagnosis

Seven things a sharp visitor notices in ten seconds.

Every point below is pulled from the live code, not a vibe. References are to real tokens and components in the repo.

01
Brand · identity

The palette belongs to everyone, so it belongs to no one.

The primary is #4f46e5 — Tailwind's indigo-600, the single most-used accent in AI-generated SaaS. Surfaces are the stock slate ramp (#f8fafc #0f172a #64748b), and nearly every surface — hero, day cells, cards — wears a 135deg linear-gradient. None of it is wrong. All of it is anonymous.

02
Brand · meaning

The name does heavy lifting the design ignores.

Hoursmith. Hour + smith — a craftsperson who forges raw material into something finished and accountable. That's exactly what the product does: it takes messy, scattered Jira worklogs and turns them into clean, invoice-ready hours. It's a fantastic name.

But the logo is a generic rounded-app-icon — an indigo square with document lines and a small amber clock — that encodes neither hours nor smithing. It would sit unnoticed among a thousand PWA icons. The metaphor is sitting right there, unused.

03
UX · narrative

The best asset on the site is buried under three card grids.

The "Why this exists" block — Bruno, a small team in Porto, every Friday at 4pm scanning the week, dreading invoice day — is the most convincing thing on the page. It's specific, human, and earns trust instantly. It's the reason someone buys.

Yet it sits below the fold, after the hero, and is followed by three generic three-up grids ("Two clear surfaces", "Why teams roll this out quickly", "Install it like an app") that say little and look templated. The emotional hook is treated as a footnote; the filler is treated as the headline.

04
Craft · color logic

The brand color and the status colors are fighting.

Here the colors carry real meaning: complete, incomplete, overtime, weekend. But "overtime" is indigo — the same hue as the primary brand and every button. So a meaningful data state and "this is a link" read as the same thing.

When semantic color is this load-bearing, the brand accent must step off the green→amber→red→indigo ramp entirely. Right now it sits on top of it.

05
UX · hierarchy

The dashboard toolbar is six equal buttons with no main action.

The dashboard's job is closing the week. But the toolbar is Pinned · Templates · Copy Prev Week · Export MD · Export CSV · ? — six variant="secondary" buttons of identical weight, plus the week navigator and a status bar. The one thing a lead came to do — fill the gaps and close — has no visual priority over a markdown export.

This is a flat-hierarchy problem, not a missing-feature problem. A "Weekly Close Assistant" component already exists in the code; the layout just doesn't let it lead.

06
Craft · typography

Hours are the whole product, but numbers get no typographic respect.

Body text is 0.875rem (14px) Inter throughout, and the hour figures — 8h, 0h, 40h / 40h — render in that same body face at body size. For a product whose entire reason to exist is making numbers trustworthy and scannable, the numerals deserve their own treatment: a confident display or mono face, tabular alignment, real scale. Right now they whisper.

07
UX · trust

The trust story is the moat — and it's under-dramatized.

"Browser-only. Your credentials only ever go to Jira. No backend." This is a genuine, rare differentiator for a security-conscious buyer — and it's delivered as one quiet line. For an enterprise / agency audience, this deserves to be a visible, designed promise (a lockup, a diagram, a repeated motif), not a sentence that scrolls by.
