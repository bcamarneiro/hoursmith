Section 02 — Directions

Three ways to make Hoursmith look like itself.

Each direction is a complete, usable system — logo, palette, type, voice, and a live application sketch — built so you can mix and match. They're not three skins of the same idea; they pull the brand toward three different feelings you flagged: distinctive, trustworthy, and friendly. The specimen for each is laid out identically so the comparison is honest.

A · ForgeBold & distinctive. The craft metaphor made tactile — hot metal, struck bars, an ember accent.
B · LedgerTrustworthy & precise. Audit-grade calm — a beautifully typeset financial instrument.
C · DaylightFriendly & calm. Invoice day without the dread — humane, warm, low-stress.

My recommendation

Forge for the soul,
Ledger for the numbers.

Forge gives Hoursmith the distinctiveness it's missing — a name-true identity nobody will mistake for a default build. But the product's promise is trust, and trust lives in the numbers. So I'd build the identity on Forge and borrow Ledger's discipline for every figure: tabular mono numerals, hairline rules, audit-grade alignment. Daylight then informs the voice and the empty/success states, so the app feels human, not severe.

IdentityForge — iron + ember, struck-bar "H", Bricolage display
NumbersLedger — Plex / JetBrains Mono, tabular, hairline rules
VoiceDaylight warmth + Forge confidence — human, never cute
StatusKeep semantic green/amber/red; ember owns the brand alone
